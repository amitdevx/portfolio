## Why Full-Stack Engineering Matters

Most engineering problems don't exist in isolation. They exist at the intersection of layers:

**Example 1**: A database query times out. Is it the query? The index? The connection pool? The infrastructure? Or the frontend requesting data incorrectly? Understanding all layers helps you actually diagnose the problem instead of guessing.

**Example 2**: Your app scales to 1M users and suddenly everything breaks. Was it the database? The API gateway? The frontend bundle size? The CI/CD pipeline? A full-stack engineer can identify real bottlenecks instead of premature optimization.

**Example 3**: You want to add AI features to your application. You need to understand cost implications (backend), latency requirements (frontend), infrastructure changes (DevOps), and integration points across the system.

Full-stack thinking prevents silos and enables much better decision-making.
